## What Usually Breaks First When Planning a Group Trip?

Group trips stall when the organiser can't get everyone to agree on dates, nail down the actual plan, or settle who owes what. Standard messaging apps like WhatsApp simply weren’t built for decision-making. General-purpose tools like Google Sheets give flexibility but leave you chasing everyone for input—and there’s always one friend who never checks the sheet. Most groups need a dedicated flow that locks in preferences fast, keeps everyone on the same page, and avoids awkward money chats.[^tryps-homepage][^google-sheets]

- Picking dates: endless polls, no commitment
- Building the itinerary: too many separate messages or docs
- Splitting costs: confusion and forgotten expenses
- Keeping everyone synced: lost in group chats

## How Does Wanderlog Compare for Planning a Trip with Friends?

Wanderlog is strong for building detailed itineraries, viewing routes on maps, and managing travel reservations. It offers group collaboration, so friends can comment or edit plans. However, Wanderlog is not built around group decision points: there’s no structured date poll or integrated expense splitting. Organisers using Wanderlog often end up running date votes in a separate group chat and shifting to Splitwise or other apps to handle money.[^wanderlog-plan-trip][^wanderlog-android]

- Detailed itinerary and map-based route planning
- Group collaboration for itinerary editing and commenting
- No built-in solution for date polling
- No expense splitting—requires an extra tool

## What About Splitwise as a Trip Planner?

Splitwise is effective for tracking who owes whom after a trip. For group travel, you can create a group and record expenses as you go—but Splitwise isn’t a planner. It doesn’t help you poll for dates, coordinate an itinerary, or replace the group chat. Most organisers using Splitwise still have to chase people for decisions in another app.[^splitwise-home]

- Great for splitting expenses at the end or during the trip
- No support for itinerary building
- No date coordination—relies on external tools
- Settling balances is separated from planning steps

## Are Google Sheets or WhatsApp Enough for Group Trip Planning?

Google Sheets gives flexibility: you can make your own poll, itinerary table, and expense columns. But Sheets requires everyone to engage, and someone to push updates and police the data. WhatsApp keeps things fast, but trip plans get buried among memes and side talk. For organisers, these tools work—until someone misses key info or you’re stuck reconciling three versions.[^google-sheets][^tryps-homepage]

- Sheets: flexible but fragmented; easy to miss votes or expenses
- WhatsApp: easy for chat, poor for capturing group decisions
- Organiser does most of the follow-up manually
- No built-in structure for group consensus

## How Does TripIt Fit in Group Trip Planning?

TripIt shines at making sense of your travel bookings. Forward confirmation emails or let it sync with your inbox, and TripIt builds a single itinerary. You can share this with friends, but collaborative planning—choosing dates, editing plans, or tracking shared costs—is out of scope. TripIt works best for one-way trip organisation, not two-way group coordination.[^tripit-how-it-works][^tripit-getting-started]

- Automatic travel itinerary creation from emails
- Easily shareable with others
- No built-in tool for group voting, itinerary editing, or expense splitting
- Best as a travel organiser, not as a planner for groups
