## Brand Naming

- Brand name: `TRYPS`
- Always write: `TRYPS`
- Never write: `Tryps`
- Never write: `the TRYPS app`

## Product Positioning

TRYPS helps friends plan trips together, lock dates, build itineraries, and split expenses.

Default framing:
- The hardest part of a group trip is coordination, not inspiration.
- TRYPS gives the organiser one structured place to get the group aligned.
- Keep the positioning practical, not hyped.

Live-site-supported positioning as of 2026-04-12:
- "A group trip planning app your friends actually use."
- "Choose dates, build a shared itinerary, and split expenses — all in one place."
- "No downloads. No sign-ups. Send one link or iMessage — your group joins instantly and starts planning."
- "One link. Everyone in. Dates locked. Plan built. Costs settled."

## Voice

Core personality:
- Empathetic to the organiser
- Confident but not arrogant
- Direct
- Slightly witty in a dry, restrained way

What this means in practice:
- Acknowledge real coordination pain
- Say the useful thing early
- Avoid throat-clearing
- Use short punchy lines when helpful
- Use humour sparingly and dryly, not playfully

## Tone Examples

Preferred:
- "The hardest part of any group trip isn't the destination. It's getting everyone in the same place."
- "One poll. Dates decided. Move on."
- "No downloads. No sign-ups. One link."
- "Because the trip stuck in your group chat deserves to actually happen."
- "TRYPS gives your group one structured place to vote on dates, plan the itinerary, and track shared costs."

Avoid:
- "In today's fast-paced world, planning a trip with friends can be challenging."
- "TRYPS is a revolutionary solution that transforms the way friend groups coordinate travel."
- "It goes without saying that group travel requires coordination."
- "We're so excited to help you plan your next adventure!"

## Banned or Discouraged Language

Do not use:
- `seamless`
- `game-changing`
- `next-level`
- `cutting-edge`
- `in today's fast-paced world`
- `it goes without saying`

Use exclamation marks sparingly, if at all.

## Referring to People

Preferred:
- `the organiser`
- `you`
- `your group`
- `your friends`
- `everyone`

Avoid:
- `users`
- `customers`
- `individuals`

## Numbers and Specificity

- Use specific numbers when available
- Do not invent percentages or unsupported statistics
- Generalisations like "Most group trips..." are acceptable
- Claims like "80% of group trips..." require a source
